Program Overview

The Driving to Success program addresses one of the most significant barriers to economic self-sufficiency for immigrant communities: transportation. In 2025, the program focused exclusively on behind-the-wheel instruction, the most expensive and difficult-to-access component of driver training. Operating three cohorts throughout the year, we partnered with Xcell Driving School to provide professional instruction with multilingual support.

Each participant received 6 hours of professional behind-the-wheel instruction at the full market rate of $395 per person, with Sanford Health's funding covering this critical expense. IDC supplemented the program with administrative support, interpreter services, and coordination costs from organizational resources.

The program received over 50 online inquiries through our intake system, with 33 participants completing training through scheduled cohorts. Approximately 20 individuals who completed intake remain on the waitlist for 2026 services. In partnership with Global Friends in Grand Forks, we extended our reach beyond the Fargo-Moorhead area, helping an additional 4 clients obtain their driver's licenses.

Program Structure and Delivery

Cohort Model

Three cohorts operated throughout 2025, allowing for structured scheduling with our driving school partner. Coordination around instructor availability required careful planning to accommodate both participant and instructor schedules.

Training Focus

6 hours of behind-the-wheel instruction per participant through Xcell Driving School. The program concentrated resources on this most expensive barrier, with IDC providing supplemental support for interpretation and coordination.

Language Support

Comprehensive multilingual services in Somali, Arabic, Swahili, Dari, Kurdish, and other languages funded through IDC's operational budget to ensure accessibility for all participants.

Success Stories

From Abandonment to Independence

A client who recently moved from Morocco on a fiancé visa found herself abandoned by her husband, who saw her empowerment as threatening. Referred to IDC by Job Service North Dakota, she received comprehensive support including immigration paperwork assistance and enrollment in Driving to Success. She passed her driver's test on the first attempt and secured employment with CVS. Her driver's license became the foundation for rebuilding her life independently, demonstrating how mobility enables economic self-sufficiency and personal empowerment.

Single Mom Finds Stability

A single mother who relocated from Arizona with a special needs child faced overwhelming challenges. Without a license, she couldn't purchase a car, making it nearly impossible to balance work schedules with her son's medical appointments and basic errands like grocery shopping. After completing 6 hours of behind-the-wheel training through Driving to Success, she obtained her license and Real ID. IDC helped her secure a reliable, affordable vehicle. Transportation independence transformed her life, enabling her to maintain employment, attend all medical appointments, and add a side gig for supplemental income.

Overcoming Physical Barriers

A participant with a hand disability had attempted to obtain her license multiple times without success, facing frustration at every turn. Our instructor from Xcell Driving School worked creatively to find adaptive solutions for her specific needs and went the extra mile by advocating for her at the DMV, helping staff understand her capabilities despite the disability. Through this personalized approach and dedicated support, she finally obtained her license. This success demonstrates how culturally competent, patient instruction combined with advocacy can overcome barriers that had previously seemed insurmountable.

Conclusion

The 2025 Driving to Success program demonstrated that removing transportation barriers creates transformative opportunities for New Americans. With a 75% success rate and 25 newly licensed drivers, the program proved that strategic investment in behind-the-wheel instruction—the most expensive barrier to licensing—directly enables economic self-sufficiency, community integration, and family stability. The partnership with Sanford Health made this impact possible. Working exclusively with Xcell Driving School allowed us to deliver consistent, quality instruction, though scheduling challenges highlighted the need for expanded instructor partnerships in 2026. Looking ahead, our planned collaboration with Fix It Forward will extend support beyond licensing to include vehicle maintenance skills and connections to affordable, reliable vehicles—completing the full spectrum of transportation independence.
